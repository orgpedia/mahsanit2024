1) The CEO / Chief Engineer will be responsible for completing all the remaining works within the prescribed time frame at the above mentioned revised cost.

3) On completion of the scheme, it should be handed over to the concerned Gram Panchayat. Immediately after the actual completion of the scheme, a joint inspection of the scheme should be conducted by the concerned Executive Engineer, Zilla Parishad and concerned Gram Panchayat officials and any defects found in such inspection should be corrected before handing over the scheme.
4) The Gram Panchayat / Zilla Parishad concerned shall not receive any assistance from the Government for meeting the recurring expenditure incurred annually for implementation of the scheme, its maintenance, repairs (including special modifications), depreciation in the scheme or for renovation of machinery.
5) On completion of the scheme, it will be mandatory for the local body (concerned Gram Panchayat / Zila Parishad) to implement the scheme. The accounts of the scheme should also be closed.
6) A CAG report in 2013 shows that 21 per cent of water supply schemes in the state have been shut down due to poor maintenance / non-payment of electricity bills. 7) Action should be taken in accordance with the prevailing policy on extortion.
8) It shall be the responsibility of the Zilla Parishad that the present scheme shall not be revised under any circumstances.
9) Since no administrative expenditure is admissible under the Jal Jeevan Mission, it should be excluded if 4% of the administrative expenditure has been taken up in the revised budget of the scheme.
10) It should be ensured at the Zilla Parishad level that the tendering process of the scheme is carried out properly.
